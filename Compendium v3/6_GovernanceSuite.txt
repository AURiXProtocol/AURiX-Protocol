AURiX GOVERNANCE SUITE — v3

(Stewardship • Succession • Territory)

0. Purpose of the Governance Suite

AURiX requires clear governance to ensure:

no single party can centralize control

no steward can modify invariants

no institution can turn AURiX into surveillance

the protocol remains safe across future hands

deployment boundaries are understood and enforced

This document defines:

Stewardship — who may operate AURiX and under what constraints

Succession — how control is handed off without capturing the protocol

Territory — where AURiX ends and external systems begin

These three layers prevent misuse and guarantee longevity.

1. Stewardship (Who Guards the Protocol)

AURiX is governed by a distributed stewardship model, not a corporation, foundation, or single owner.

1.1 Steward Requirements

A steward must:

run AURiX in full compliance with LPIN and SELFiX

publish any changes to non-core components

maintain transparent build processes

agree to independent replay audits

refuse identity collapse, personalization, or inference

uphold anti-capture norms

Stewards can be:

browser vendors

standards bodies

NGO coalitions

research institutions

open-source councils

regulated public infrastructure organizations

No steward may unilaterally steer AURiX.

1.2 Stewardship Responsibilities

Stewards are required to:

Enforce invariants

Provide transparent builds

Maintain replay compatibility

Uphold SELFiX boundaries

Conduct periodic safety reviews

Prevent misuse by downstream implementers

They do not control:

product decisions

UI layers (Y-Lens)

enterprise deployments

third-party adaptations

the philosophical framing of attestation

They govern the protocol, not the ecosystem.

1.3 Stewardship Limitations

A steward may not:

modify invariants

weaken LPIN or SELFiX

introduce telemetry

create identity-linked attestation

create personalized or persuasive outputs

convert attestation into scoring or ranking

restrict who may implement AURiX

extract data from AURiX instances

Violation → immediate suspension from the stewardship network.

2. Succession (How AURiX Moves Beyond Any Single Person)

This section solves the fatal flaw in most open protocols:
they silently centralize over time.

AURiX succession rules guarantee continuity and anti-capture.

2.1 Succession Trigger Conditions

Succession occurs when:

The original architect steps back

A steward leaves

A steward is removed

New institutional partners join

A governance deadlock occurs

Version changes require cross-steward alignment

Succession is routine, not dramatic.

2.2 Succession Process

Any steward may propose a succession event.

All stewards must run a replay audit to confirm protocol integrity.

Stewardship roles shift to maintain distributed representation.

No new steward may introduce identity-based modifications.

Succession is complete only when all invariants are re-affirmed.

The protocol is allowed to evolve — but invariants never change.

2.3 Successor Eligibility

Eligible successors must:

uphold LPIN

uphold SELFiX

have no conflict of interest

maintain public transparency

agree to replay audits

refuse identity-collapse systems

commit to anti-capture governance

AURiX cannot be inherited by:

companies with advertising motives

governments with surveillance motives

platforms with personalization incentives

AI labs whose models require user profiling

This protects the future.

3. Territory (Where AURiX Ends)

Territory defines the boundary of responsibility.

AURiX cannot be held accountable for what lies outside its territory — and nothing outside may alter what lies inside.

3.1 AURiX Territory

AURiX governs:

invariants

LPIN posture

SELFiX duality

attestation logic

replay contract

the behavior of primitives

safety states

ledger structure

internal protocol stack operations

Everything within this boundary is canonical.

3.2 Outside AURiX Territory

These do not belong to AURiX:

UI rendering (Y-Lens)

business logic

enterprise identity policies

application-level decisions

downstream interpretation

regulatory frameworks

design systems

user experience choices

analytics

content moderation

content understanding

trust scoring

predictive AI

cloud infrastructure

This prevents:

blame misplacement

regulatory confusion

vendor capture

protocol abuse

privacy violations

If it’s outside the territory, AURiX cannot be blamed for it — and cannot be forced to change to accommodate it.

3.3 Territorial Hard Boundary

If a steward merges AURiX logic with:

identity-based systems

inference engines

behavioral scoring

reputation networks

remote telemetry

advertising systems

→ The product is not AURiX and cannot use the name.

3.4 Territorial Soft Boundary

AURiX can be used next to other systems as long as boundaries remain intact.

Examples:

Y-Lens UI

enterprise dashboards

auditor consoles

research tools

regulatory overlays

These may visualize AURiX — but may not redefine it.

4. Consensus & Versioning
4.1 Invariants Cannot Be Versioned

They are permanent.

4.2 Surface Definitions May Evolve

But must retain replay compatibility.

4.3 Stewards Must Agree on Version Changes

And any steward may veto identity-risky proposals.

4.4 Replay Must Validate Old and New Versions

Backward compatibility is mandatory.

5. Governance Failure Modes

AURiX fails safely if:

A steward attempts capture

An institution demands identity collapse

A platform requests targeted inference

A regulator asks for surveillance access

A third-party weakens the invariants

A version update breaks replay

Failure mode → global HALT, meaning:

attestation ceases

ledger entries freeze

replay sessions disallow forward validation

stewards are alerted

misuse is contained

This is how AURiX prevents harm at scale.

6. Stewardship Summary (One Paragraph)

AURiX stewardship is distributed, anti-capture, invariant-preserving, and identity-neutral.
Stewards maintain the protocol, not the ecosystem.
Succession ensures longevity without centralization.
Territory ensures that AURiX cannot be misused or blamed for non-protocol logic.
Together, these constraints make AURiX safe to scale globally.

7. Single-Sentence Summary

AURiX governance ensures that no one can own the protocol, no one can weaken its protections, and no implementation can violate identity, privacy, or safety — now or in any future succession.
