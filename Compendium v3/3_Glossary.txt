AURiX GLOSSARY — v3
AURiX

A local-only, privacy-preserving behavioral protocol that produces structural attestation states without inferring identity, predicting intent, collapsing users into clusters, or exporting behavioral data.
AURiX enforces LPIN, SELFiX, anti-capture constraints, and strict invariants via a reproducible, local-only protocol stack.

Attestation

A structural confirmation of behavioral stability under invariant tests.
Not a reward, not a score, not a judgment.
AURiX defines three attestation states: Stable, Uncertain, Unsafe.

Attestation Engine

The layer of the AURiX protocol stack that converts normalized behavioral vectors into one of the three attestation states.
It cannot output content, explanation, or guidance — only state.

Attested Benefit

A positive outcome recognized by the protocol without implying correctness, truth, merit, morality, or identity.
Indicates only that behavior remained stable and non-contradictory within the invariants.

Boundary-Layer Filter

A layer that strips away identity, personal content, and environmental noise from raw local signals, producing a clean behavioral delta.
It ensures that no signal entering the invariant tests contains user-specific data.

Behavioral Delta

A normalized, identity-free measurement of change in local behavior.
Derived only from allowed surfaces (e.g., contradiction rate, drift, stability).

Capture

Any attempt by a person, company, government, ideology, or AI system to centralize control, telemetry, identity, or inference over AURiX.
If capture occurs, the implementation is not AURiX.

Contradiction

A detectable mismatch between two or more behavioral surfaces, such as inconsistent patterns or fluctuating structural signals.
Contradiction is non-moral and non-predictive — it is a metric of inconsistency.

Drift

A rate of change in behavioral patterns over time.
Drift is not “bad” — it merely indicates decreasing stability.
High drift → attestation shifts toward Uncertain.

External SELFiX

The observable layer of behavior AURiX may analyze.
Includes contradictions, drift, boundary-layer signals, and surface-level behavior.
Never includes identity or private content.

FLIBD (Full-Layer Invariant Behavioral Diagnostics)

A non-political, non-ideological diagnostic method for evaluating cross-system behavioral contradictions and drift.
AURiX uses FLIBD as an external reference lens for validating attestation consistency across large systems.

Halt

A forced stop in attestation triggered by an invariant violation.
Halt is not punishment — it is a protective behavior.

Identity Collapse

Any attempt to predict, profile, cluster, infer, categorize, or otherwise “sum up” a human or system based on behavior.
Strictly forbidden.
AURiX prevents this through LPIN and SELFiX.

Invariant

A non-negotiable protocol requirement.
If violated, AURiX must halt or isolate.
Examples: Locality, Privacy, Identity Non-Collapse, Non-Persuasion, Anti-Extraction, Reproducibility.

Invariant Test

A deterministic check used to verify that the invariants have not been violated.
Run on every cycle.
Must be reproducible locally.

LPIN (Local-Only, Privacy-Preserving, Identity-Noncollapsing)

The foundational posture of AURiX.
Ensures the protocol:

never leaves the device,

never stores or exports private data,

never reduces humans to interpreted identities.

Ledger (Local Ledger)

A minimal, privacy-preserving structure that records attestation events for replay verification.
Contains no user data, no history, no identity, and no reconstructable behavior.

Method Physics

AURiX’s underlying theoretical framework derived from contradiction, drift, reproducibility, and invariant dynamics.
Designed for behavioral analysis without relying on identity or inference.

Normalization

The process of converting raw behavioral signals into stable, identity-free deltas suitable for invariant testing.

Pattern Consistency

A measure of how stable or repeatable an observed behavior is.
Higher consistency → closer to Stable attestation.

Replay Contract

A formal agreement inside the protocol defining how attestation states can be reproduced locally.
Used for audits and validation.
Replay cannot reconstruct identity, only the logic that produced the attestation.

SELFiX (Self-Identity Exclusion Architecture)

A dual-structured identity protection mechanism that ensures:

AURiX only sees external behavior (permitted),

AURiX never touches internal identity (forbidden).

SELFiX prevents personalization, inference, and collapse.

Stable State

Attestation surface indicating that behavior is predictable and contradiction-free under invariant tests.

Uncertain State

Attestation surface indicating drift, instability, or contradiction that is not unsafe but not stable.

Unsafe State

Attestation surface indicating an invariant violation, triggering HALT.

Y-Lens

The implementation-layer expression of AURiX attestation (UI, cursor, halos), representing the protocol’s outputs in real-world applications.
Y-Lens is not the protocol — it is the visualization and interface built on top of it.

Territory

The boundary defining where AURiX ends and external systems begin.
Ensures that AURiX is not held responsible for downstream or upstream interpretations.

Traffic-Light Equivalent

The common UI expression of attestation states (green / yellow / red).
Not required by the protocol; merely a conventional representation.

Zero-State

The neutral position before any attestation occurs.
No inference, no signals, no assumptions.
